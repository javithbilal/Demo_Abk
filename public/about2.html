About Us

Welcome to ABK-AOTS BANGLORE


    • ABK AOTS DOSOKAI Tamil Nadu centre was started in the year 1973 by Mr M R Ranganathan, the Founder Chairman. His vast experience with Japanese clients and his in-depth understanding of the Japanese culture and tradition helped in the rapid growth of the organisation.

      ABK AOTS DOSOKAI, Japanese Language school, Bangalore Chapter was started on the 29th of May 2017. This is the first franchise outside of Tamil Nadu. We work closely with the Consulate General of Japan, Bengaluru and we are also an active member of Indo Japanese Chamber of Commerce and Industries, Bengaluru.

      Every language has its beauty and tradition and the Japanese language has an added advantage of having a lot of similarities with Indian languages and culture.

      Our students are a heterogeneous mix of working professionals, students, home makers and retired people with the age group ranging from 16 to 60.

      The classes are conducted by a team of skillful and dedicated teachers, trained by the Japan Foundation.

      We offer online and classroom sessions both on weekdays and weekends as per the convenience of the students. Our online classes are particularly popular with professionals and students outside India.

      We specialize in The Fast Track courses and short-term conversational courses. These are preferred by people who need to make short duration visit to Japan on official engagements.

      The books and course material are the ones prescribed by the Japan Foundation for JLPT /NAT. The teaching methods focus on improving the Listening, Speaking, Reading and Writing skills of students. The various tools used for teaching include Flashcards for Kanji, Kanji Games, Roleplays, audio and visual aids. These along with the well-equipped library help in enhancing the overall language proficiency of students.

      We conduct periodical tests for assessments. Our institute specializes on intense Revision and Mock tests. These go a long way in enhancing the confidence of students before the exams.
